2. Executive Summary

CreateUp is an AI-powered platform that takes a YouTube creator from a raw idea to a ready-to-film, long-form script in roughly twelve minutes. It combines three pillars that most competing tools treat as separate products: (1) a competitive-intelligence database of indexed YouTube channels and videos, (2) a conversational ideation and research workspace wired directly into YouTube data, and (3) a structured, voice-aware script-writing canvas that produces scripts up to three hours / 30,000 words long.

Unlike generic AI chatbots that produce short, generic copy, CreateUp is built around proven YouTube storytelling frameworks — hooks, curiosity gaps, retention structures, and outlier-driven idea generation. Every script is shaped by a per-channel Voice Profile and Audience Avatar so output sounds like the creator, not a robot.

This document specifies the full feature set required to reproduce and ship CreateUp: onboarding, channels, voice and audience modeling, the Intel research database, outlier-based idea generation, ideation chat, the script canvas, an automated Agent Mode pipeline, AI thumbnail generation, templates, research management, team collaboration, role-based multi-user access, multi-model AI routing, and optional API/MCP access.


North-star metric: median time from idea to exported first-draft script ≤ 12 minutes, at ≥ 50% projected viewer-retention quality.

Beyond research and scripting, CreateUp also manages the downstream workflow: moving each piece of content through writing, filming and editing stages; tracking tasks, assignments and a publishing calendar; organizing B-roll and visual inspiration; documenting repeatable team processes (SOPs); collecting audience-submitted ideas; and closing the loop with published-video performance. The result is a single workspace for the whole content lifecycle — not just the script.



3. Product Vision & Goals


3.1 Vision

Become the operating system for YouTube content creation — where creators (and teams) research what works, generate validated ideas, and produce on-brand scripts and packaging (titles, thumbnails) without leaving one workspace.


3.2 Goals


Speed: Compress the idea→script cycle from hours/days to minutes.


Quality: Produce scripts that apply storytelling structure and the creator’s authentic voice, requiring light human polish rather than rewriting.


Validation: Ground every idea in real performance data (outlier videos) instead of guesswork.


Breadth: Cover the whole pre-production workflow: research, ideation, scripting, packaging, and export.


Scale: Support solo creators through multi-client agencies via channels, multi-user workspaces, and roles.


3.3 Non-Goals (v1)


Video editing / NLE: we track editing stages (Edit Bay) but do not edit footage or replace a video editor.


Auto-publishing: we sync published stats and export metadata/chapter markers, but do not auto-upload to YouTube.


Auto-scheduling/posting: we provide a planning content calendar and generate promo copy, but do not auto-post to social platforms.


Native mobile apps (responsive web is sufficient for v1).


Sponsor / brand-deal pipeline and multi-format course/book production are deferred (see Section 17).

5. Glossary & Domain Concepts
Term
Definition
Channel
A project workspace holding one creator/series’ voice, audience, competitors, scripts, ideas, research and settings. A user may own several.
Voice Profile
A structured model of how a channel’s scripts should sound, trained from top videos and editable in Simple or Advanced mode.
Audience Avatar
A detailed profile of the ideal viewer (demographics, psychographics, behaviors, key questions) plus a representative photo.
Intel
The competitive-intelligence database of 100,000+ indexed YouTube channels/videos with search, filters and analytics.
Outlier Score
Ratio of a video’s views to the average of up to 10 surrounding videos on the same channel (e.g. 5x = five times the channel norm).
Velocity Score
Proprietary momentum metric flagging channels growing rapidly relative to their size.
Views/Sub Ratio
How far a video reached beyond its channel’s subscriber base; high = strong discovery.
Idea
A generated video concept: title, topic, strategy (the hook/why-it-works), source outlier video, and suggested length.
Canvas
The split-panel script workspace: chat on the left, Plan/Script editor on the right.
Template
A structure/pacing framework (e.g. Educational, Documentary) applied when generating a script.
Agent Mode
An automated pipeline that researches, outlines, writes and QA-checks a full script unattended.
Script Builder (Classic)
An alternative, structured step-by-step scripting workflow (Research → Frame → Title → Thumbnail → Hook → Payoffs → Draft → Edit → Export → Publish) with section-level regeneration.
AI Research
A deep, multi-source research tool (distinct from a quick web search) that synthesizes a referenceable report into the research library.
Workspace
The shared container for a set of users, channels and resources. Members sign in individually and hold a Role. (No billing/credits.)
Channel Memory
Persisted facts/preferences the AI should remember and reuse across scripts in a channel.
Content Project
A single piece of content (video, etc.) tracked from idea through publish, with a Status and Edit Status.
Production Pipeline
Stage-focused work queues — Writer’s Room (research/writing), Film Queue (recording), Edit Bay (editing) — that filter content by its current stage.
Edit Status
Post-production sub-stage of a project: Assembly, Rough cut, VFX, Sound & music, Color grading.
B-Roll / Shotlist
A reusable library of shots, stock, animations and assets, importable from editor markers and linkable to projects.
Swipe File
A library of visual inspiration (thumbnails, set design, landing pages) captured via a web clipper or upload.
Wiki / SOP
Human-facing process documentation, standard operating procedures and checklists, optionally scoped per channel.
Content Calendar
A scheduled view of planned and in-progress content by publish date.
Idea Merit
A tag classifying an idea’s strategic role (e.g. Content Pillar, Trending Topic, Experiment).
Audience Submission
A topic/idea suggested by a viewer via a public intake form.

15. Assumptions, Dependencies & Risks


15.1 Assumptions


Public YouTube data and transcripts are accessible at the scale required for Intel and voice/audience training.


Third-party LLM/image/voice providers remain available with acceptable latency and cost.


As the system is free to users, the operator absorbs AI/provider cost; optional admin soft limits (Section 13.2) keep that cost bounded.


15.2 Key Risks & Mitigations

16. Acceptance Criteria Approach

Each requirement is verified by acceptance tests written in Given/When/Then form, traced by Req ID. A feature is Done when its Must-priority requirements pass, telemetry is wired (NFR-OBS), and the relevant screen matches the approved mockup. Example:

FR-CANV-11 (Humanize): Given a generated script, When the user clicks Humanize, Then the rewrite streams section-by-section, the voice profile is preserved, AI-pattern phrases are reduced, and readability targets ~6th–7th grade — at no cost and with no regeneration cap.



17. Future / Phase 2+


Autonomous channel agents: agents that proactively grow a channel (research, plan, draft, package) and ask for feedback at checkpoints.


Full video production: end-to-end voiceover → avatar → rendered video from a finished script.


Direct YouTube publishing & analytics loop: push exports and read back performance to refine ideas/voice.


Native mobile apps and offline teleprompter.
